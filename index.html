About Tigers

The tiger (Pantherna Trigis) is the world's most biggest cat species,and mostly recognizable
for the pattern on the black striped stripes on their orange fur.
The other animals in the big cat family are lions,leopards jaguar,and snow leopards.

Tigers are the meridian of predators.They hunt mammals like deers and bovids.
Tigers are endangered animals because many people like to hunt tigers for their skin.

Features

Tigers are very strong and have powerful musels.They have big heads and long tails.
They use their black stripes for camouflouge with grass that makes them sneaky and hard to see.
Tigers are one of the only cats with black stripes.

Habitat

Countries with large tiger population

Some countries with large tiger populations include India,Russia,Indonesia,Malasia,Nepal and Thailand.

Countries with small tiger population

Some countries with smaller tiger populations include Bangladesh,Bhutan,China and Laos.
